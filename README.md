# Semantic Noise

## Definition

Semantic Noise is the presence of inconsistent, duplicated, or misaligned signals within a digital system that weakens its interpretability by AI systems.

---

## Explanation

Semantic Noise occurs when a system contains:

- similar content expressed in different ways without adding new perspective  
- duplicated or near-identical articles without real added value  
- inconsistent descriptions of the same concept across pages  
- content created without a clear thematic direction  

From a human perspective, each element may seem valid and justified.

From an AI perspective, these signals become:

- fragmented  
- inconsistent  
- weak  

The system does not perceive depth or nuance.

It perceives ambiguity.

---

## In Generative Engine Optimization (GEO)

In the context of Generative Engine Optimization, Semantic Noise represents one of the main causes of misinterpretation.

AI systems do not read pages individually.  
They analyze systems.

When the system contains too many variations of the same concept, the AI cannot determine which version is the most relevant.

---

## Effect on AI Interpretation

When Semantic Noise is present:

- distinctions between concepts are lost  
- positioning becomes unclear  
- signals lose strength  

At that point, the system does not try to resolve complexity.

It simplifies.

---

## Final Outcome

The result is a reduced and generic representation.

What was specific becomes general.

What was distinctive becomes interchangeable.

---

## Note

"Semantic Noise" is not a standardized technical term, but a conceptual model used to describe a recurring pattern in how AI systems interpret inconsistent information.
